01

03
The NRI 8.1% punches far above its count. 268 overseas profiles + ~200 Return NRIs = ~14% of the market has significant international exposure. This segment drives premium positioning and disproportionate search activity.
04
STEM educated, but the career pivot is real. 53.4% STEM education but engineering-to-non-engineering career pivots are common (55% shift). Education and occupation labels tell different stories.
05
Lifestyle homogeneity is almost total. 98.2% non-smoker, 91.3% non-drinker, 64.7% vegetarian. This market is not differentiated by lifestyle — you must go deeper (sub-caste, city, career) to find signal.
06
The horoscope layer is noise for filtering, signal for compatibility. Rashi is near-perfectly flat (no dominant sign). Nadi is three equal thirds. Nakshatra covers all 27. Horoscope doesn't pre-filter the market — it operates as compatibility chemistry at the individual level.
07
Medical professionals (285) are the most premium occupation cluster. 8.6% of market, high stability, defined career arc. Doctor/Dentist combination is a structurally distinct sub-market — different timelines, different expectations.
08
The platform is underrepresenting its highest-value profiles. PhD (15), Scientists (10), Return NRIs (~200), Research Fellows (43) — all high-quality signals, all structurally underrepresented. The best profiles may not be the most visible ones.
09
Upper Middle Class is the market's natural self-description. 47% UMC + 5.6% Affluent + 1% Elite = majority sees themselves as "above average." The 291 who declined to disclose likely skew up, not down. Wealth aspiration is market-wide.
10
The 80/20 of this market: Deshastha (62%) × Maharashtra-resident (90% of India-based) × Post-Graduate (51%) × Private/MNC (67%) × Veg + Non-smoker (majority) = a remarkably homogeneous core. The interesting market lives in the 20% edges.
